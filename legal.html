User Responsibilities

As a user of FLO, you are responsible for:

  • Verifying all tax calculations with a qualified tax professional before filing
  • Ensuring compliance with current IRS regulations, state tax laws, and local ordinances
  • Maintaining accurate financial records as required by law
  • Reviewing all estimates and reports before making financial decisions
  • Consulting with certified public accountants (CPAs), enrolled agents (EAs), or tax attorneys for official tax preparation and advice
  • Understanding that tax laws change frequently and app data may not reflect the most current regulations
  • Keeping original receipts and documentation as required by tax authorities
  • Meeting all tax deadlines - our reminders are helpful but not guaranteed to be accurate or complete

Tax Estimate Limitations

Tax estimates provided by FLO are based on:

  • Self-reported transaction data entered by users (garbage in, garbage out)
  • Generalized federal tax brackets and standard deductions
  • Approximate state tax rates (using statewide averages)
  • IRS guidance and tax rates current as of the app's last update
  • Simplified calculations designed for general planning purposes

What Our Estimates Do NOT Account For

These estimates may not account for:

  • Complex tax situations such as:
    • Investment income (capital gains, dividends, interest)
    • Real estate transactions or rental income
    • Foreign income or foreign bank accounts
    • Business partnerships, S-corps, or C-corps
    • Stock options or restricted stock units
    • Inheritance or estate tax considerations
  • Recent changes to tax law not yet incorporated into the app
  • State or local tax variations beyond general rates
  • Individual deductions, credits, or special circumstances including:
    • Mortgage interest deduction
    • Student loan interest deduction
    • Child tax credits or earned income credits
    • Education credits (American Opportunity, Lifetime Learning)
    • Retirement contribution deductions beyond SEP IRA basics
    • Health savings account (HSA) contributions
  • Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) considerations
  • Net investment income tax (3.8% Medicare surtax)
  • Additional Medicare tax on high earners
  • Estimated tax payment penalties and safe harbor calculations beyond the basic rules
  • Multi-state tax situations if you work in multiple states
  • Passive activity losses or hobby loss rules

Mileage Tracking Limitations

While FLO provides GPS-based mileage tracking, you acknowledge that:

  • GPS accuracy varies based on signal strength, weather, device quality, and environmental factors
  • The IRS requires contemporaneous records - you should review and verify trips promptly
  • The IRS requires documentation of: date, destination, business purpose, and miles driven
  • GPS logs alone may not satisfy IRS requirements - you must document the business purpose
  • You are responsible for classifying trips as business vs. personal
  • The standard mileage rate changes annually - ensure the app is updated
  • Some trip types may require actual expense method instead of standard mileage rate

Receipt Management Limitations

Receipt scanning uses OCR technology which has limitations:

  • OCR accuracy depends on receipt quality, lighting, and image clarity
  • You must verify all extracted data - do not rely on OCR blindly
  • The IRS requires original receipts or acceptable substitutes - digital images may not always be sufficient
  • Receipts can fade over time - store originals properly
  • For purchases over certain thresholds, additional documentation may be required
